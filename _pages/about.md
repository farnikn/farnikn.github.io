I'm an [NSF GRFP](https://www.nsfgrfp.org) fellow and a Ph.D. candidate in Cosmology at [University of Pennsylvania](https://www.physics.upenn.edu). My research is focussed on large-scale structures (LSS) of the Universe. This involves studying cosmological structures on the scales of galaxies and larger to help our understanding of formation and evolution of the Universe. I work with [Ravi K. Sheth](https://www.physics.upenn.edu/index.php/people/standing-faculty/ravi-sheth) on the Baryon Acoustic Oscillation (BAO) standard ruler; develop physical models and statistical methods which allow the data from large scale surveys constrain cosmological models. Prior to Penn, I received my B.Sc. in Physics (with a Minor in Computer Science) at the [Sharif University of Technology](http://www.en.sharif.edu), where I worked with [Shant Baghram](http://sharif.edu/~baghram/) on the stochastic aspects of dark matter halo formation.
